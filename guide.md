# Rubric for Documentation Review

Copy \+ paste the appropriate markdown sections into Github, and fill it out.  
You can check boxes by inserting an x into the brackets (e.g., \- \[x\])

# Quick Review

This rubric is designed for a quick review of documentation when you are limited on time or if you are reviewing a moderate edit to the documentation.

`## Quick review`

`### Checks`  
`- [ ] Introduction section is included and states the goal of the page`  
`- [ ] Table of Contents w/ working links`

`### Who is the intended audience?`  
`- [ ] General CHTC users (beginner-intermediate)`  
`- [ ] Beginner users`  
`- [ ] Intermediate users`  
`- [ ] Proficient users`

`### Quick questions`  
`**How long did it take you to go through this guide?**`  
`**Does the purpose of the guide match the contents?**`  
`**Is the table of contents comprehensive and accurate?**`  
`**Did you need other information to help you finish the tutorial/read the guide?**`  
`**Is the way the information organized logical?**`  
`**Is the information accurate?**`

`### Writing style`  
`- [ ] Is there a consistent active voice? (subject-verb-object sentence structure)`  
`- [ ] Are the words simple and concrete (vs. fancy and abstract)?`  
`- [ ] Is the tone informal, friendly, and sympathetic?`  
`- [ ] Is second-person used when possible? (cookbook style vs third-person)`  
`- [ ] Is the documentation written in present-tense?`  
`- [ ] Are the terms correct? Are new terms defined?`  
`- [ ] Are the transitions between sections effective?`
